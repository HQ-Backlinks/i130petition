What is the I-130 Petition?

The I-130 petition is the first step in the family-based immigration process. It allows U.S. citizens and lawful permanent residents (green card holders) to sponsor eligible family members to immigrate to the United States. This petition is filed with U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS) and is essential for bringing family members together under one roof.

Who Can File the I-130 Petition?

  • U.S. citizens can file an I-130 petition for their spouse, children, parents, and siblings.

  • Permanent residents can file for their spouse and unmarried children.

Once approved, the petition allows the sponsored family member to apply for a green card (permanent residency) and begin their journey toward legal residency in the U.S.

The I-130 Petition Process

Filing the I-130 petition involves several key steps, all of which we guide our clients through:

    1. Determine Eligibility

    The first step is confirming that you are eligible to sponsor your family member under U.S. immigration law. We will help you determine if your relationship meets the requirements for the I-130 petition.

    2. Prepare the Petition

    We assist in gathering the necessary documents, including proof of your U.S. citizenship or permanent residency, proof of the family relationship, and other supporting documents to ensure a strong application.

    3. Submit the Petition

    Once the petition is complete, we handle the submission to USCIS and monitor the status of your case. We ensure that everything is filed correctly to avoid unnecessary delays or requests for additional information.

    4. Approval and Next Steps

    After USCIS reviews and approves the petition, we guide your family member through the next steps, whether they are applying for a visa from abroad or adjusting their status if they are already in the U.S.
